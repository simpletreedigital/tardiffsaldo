Why You Need a Trust, Not Just a Will

A will goes through probate. That means a California court oversees the distribution of your assets, a process that can take 12 to 18 months and cost thousands of dollars in court fees, all while your family waits. A properly funded living trust sidesteps that entirely. Your assets transfer directly to your beneficiaries, privately, without court involvement.

At Tardiff & Saldo Law Offices, we've sat on both sides of this issue. We've drafted estate plans, and we've litigated the poorly written ones. That firsthand experience shapes every document we create for clients in San Luis Obispo and across SLO County.

California has specific rules around community property, Prop 19 reassessment, and trust administration that require careful drafting. Generic online forms don't account for these nuances. A local estate planning attorney who practices in California courts does.

Estate Planning Services We Provide

Our full estate plan covers everything most families need to protect their assets and their loved ones. Each package is customized to your situation.

  • Revocable Living Trusts: Avoid probate, protect privacy, and control how assets are distributed. The cornerstone of most estate plans we create.
  • Pour-Over Wills: Catch any assets not transferred to your trust during your lifetime and direct them appropriately.
  • Powers of Attorney: Designate someone you trust to manage financial decisions if you become incapacitated.
  • Healthcare Directives & Advance Health Care Directives: Specify your wishes for medical treatment and name a healthcare agent.
  • HIPAA Authorizations: Allow designated individuals to access your medical records when it matters most.
  • Special Needs Trusts: Protect a beneficiary with disabilities without jeopardizing their government benefit eligibility.
  • Guardianship Designations: Name who will care for your minor children if something happens to you.
  • Estate Tax Planning: Strategies to minimize the impact of estate taxes for high-asset families.

We also handle probate administration when a loved one passes without a trust, or when the trust requires court involvement. If you're navigating an existing estate, we can review the plan and advise on what needs to be updated. Contact us for a free review.

Estate Planning FAQs

Do I really need a trust if I already have a will?+
A will alone means probate. In California, probate is a court-supervised process that can take a year or more and cost thousands of dollars in court and attorney fees. A properly funded revocable living trust avoids probate entirely, keeps your affairs private, and allows assets to transfer immediately to your beneficiaries without court involvement. Most families in San Luis Obispo County benefit from having both a trust and a pour-over will as a backstop.
How much does a living trust cost in California?+
We offer flat-fee estate planning packages that include a complete plan: living trust, pour-over will, power of attorney, healthcare directive, and HIPAA authorization. We share full pricing during your free consultation with no surprises. Payment plans are available. The cost of not having a trust, measured in probate fees and delays, far exceeds the cost of planning ahead.
What happens if I die without a trust in California?+
Your estate goes through probate court. The process is public, time-consuming, and expensive. California statutory probate fees are set by law and are based on the gross value of your estate, not its net value. A $700,000 home with a $500,000 mortgage is still a $700,000 estate for probate fee purposes. This is one of the most common estate planning mistakes we see.
How long does it take to set up a trust?+
For most clients, we can draft and finalize a complete estate plan within 2 to 4 weeks. We start with your free consultation, gather the details we need, draft the documents, and walk you through signing and funding your trust. Funding, meaning transferring assets into the trust's name, is a step many attorneys skip that we handle with you directly.
Can you help if I already have an old trust?+
Yes. Tax laws, family circumstances, and assets change over time. A trust drafted more than 10 years ago may not reflect current California law or your current wishes. We review existing plans and advise on whether updates are needed, without charging for a new plan you don't need.
What is a special needs trust and when do I need one?+
A special needs trust allows you to leave assets to a beneficiary with a disability without disqualifying them from government benefits like Medi-Cal or SSI. If you leave assets directly to someone receiving these benefits, the inheritance can disqualify them from critical programs. A properly drafted special needs trust lets you provide for them without that consequence. This is one area where generic planning fails and local expertise matters.
