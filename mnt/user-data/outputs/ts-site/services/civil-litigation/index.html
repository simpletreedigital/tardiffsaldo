Civil Litigation FAQs

How long do I have to file a personal injury claim in California?+
California's statute of limitations for most personal injury claims is two years from the date of injury. However, there are important exceptions. Claims against government entities (cities, counties, CalTrans) require a government tort claim within six months of the incident. Claims for minors may be tolled until they turn 18. Medical malpractice has a different timeline. Do not wait to consult an attorney, earlier is always better for evidence preservation and case strength.
How much does a civil litigation attorney cost?+
For personal injury cases, we work on a contingency fee basis, meaning you pay no attorney fees unless we recover compensation for you. For business disputes and other civil matters, we discuss billing structures during your free consultation. We're transparent about fees upfront so there are no surprises.
What should I do immediately after a car accident in San Luis Obispo?+
Call 911 if anyone is injured. Seek medical attention even if you feel fine, as symptoms from whiplash and other injuries can appear days later. Document the scene with photos if you can. Exchange insurance and contact information with the other driver. Do not give a recorded statement to any insurance company without speaking to an attorney first. Then contact us for a free case review. What you say to insurers in the first days can significantly affect your claim.
Will my case go to trial?+
Most civil cases settle before trial, but the credibility to go to trial is what creates settlement leverage. Insurance companies and defense attorneys evaluate opposing counsel carefully. When they know your attorney has actual trial experience in local courts, they take the case more seriously from the outset. We never push clients to settle for less than their case is worth, and we're fully prepared to take a case to verdict when that's what it takes.
