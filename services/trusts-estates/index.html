Estate Planning in San Luis Obispo

Why You Need a Trust, Not Just a Will

A will goes through probate. In California that process can take 12 to 18 months and cost thousands of dollars in statutory fees calculated on the gross value of your estate. A properly funded living trust sidesteps all of that. Your assets transfer directly to your beneficiaries, privately, without any court involvement.

At Tardiff & Saldo, we have drafted estate plans and we have litigated the poorly written ones. That experience shapes every document we create for clients across SLO County.

What a Complete Estate Plan Includes

  • Revocable Living Trust: The cornerstone of most estate plans. Avoids probate, protects privacy, controls how assets are distributed.
  • Pour-Over Will: Catches any assets not transferred to your trust during your lifetime.
  • Durable Power of Attorney: Names someone to manage your finances if you become incapacitated.
  • Advance Health Care Directive: Specifies your medical wishes and names a healthcare agent.
  • HIPAA Authorization: Allows designated people to access your medical records when it matters.
  • Special Needs Trusts: Protects a beneficiary with disabilities without affecting their government benefits.
  • Guardianship Designations: Names who will care for your minor children if something happens to you.

Estate Planning FAQs

Do I really need a trust if I already have a will?+
A will alone means probate. In California, probate can take over a year and cost thousands in court fees calculated on your estate's gross value. A properly funded revocable living trust avoids probate entirely, keeps your affairs private, and allows assets to transfer directly to your beneficiaries without court involvement.
How much does a living trust cost in California?+
We offer flat-fee estate planning packages covering the living trust, pour-over will, power of attorney, healthcare directive, and HIPAA authorization. Full pricing is shared during your free consultation with no surprises. Payment plans are available.
What happens if I die without a trust in California?+
Your estate goes through probate court. The process is public, time-consuming, and expensive. California statutory fees are based on the gross value of your estate, not its net value. A $700,000 home with a $500,000 mortgage is still a $700,000 estate for probate fee purposes.
Can I use an online service to create my trust?+
Online services generate template documents but don't ensure California-specific compliance, walk you through trust funding, or address Prop 19 implications for property owners. We see the aftermath of DIY estate planning regularly, often when it is too late to fix problems without costly court proceedings.
