Dog Bite Law in San Luis Obispo

What California Strict Liability Means for You

California Civil Code section 3342 holds dog owners strictly liable for bites that occur in public or when the victim was lawfully present on private property. No proof of negligence is required, and no prior bite history is needed. If you were bitten while walking, running, visiting a home, or using a public space in the San Luis Obispo area, the owner is almost certainly liable under state law.

The Central Coast is an active, outdoor community. El chorro regional park, laguna lake dog park, islay hill open space, and the bob jones city to sea trail are popular destinations for residents and visitors, and dog attacks happen across all of these settings. Attacks occur across the city, including on neighborhood streets near Cal Poly, along the Broad Street and Foothill Boulevard corridors, and on trails throughout the Irish Hills and Johnson Ranch open spaces. Once an attack occurs, the critical question is not usually whether the owner is liable under California law. It is whether you have representation that documents your damages fully and negotiates a settlement that reflects the real cost of what happened, or files suit in San Luis Obispo County Superior Court if the insurer won't settle fairly.

What to Do After a Dog Attack in the San Luis Obispo Area

  • Get medical care immediately. Go to French Hospital Medical Center at 1911 Johnson Avenue or Sierra Vista Regional Medical Center. Dog bite wounds can become infected within 24 to 48 hours. The medical record created at treatment is the foundation of your claim.
  • Report the bite. Contact SLO County Animal Services at (805) 781-4400. An official report creates a record and may reveal prior incidents with the same dog, which can affect the overall case.
  • Document your injuries. Photograph your wounds immediately, every day for the first week, and periodically through your recovery. Photograph the location where the attack occurred. Get names and contact information from witnesses.
  • Do not give a recorded statement to the owner's insurance company. They will contact you quickly. You are not required to speak with them before you have legal representation, and what you say can reduce what you ultimately recover.

What is California's strict liability dog bite rule?+
California Civil Code section 3342 makes dog owners liable for bites that occur in public or when the victim was lawfully present on private property. No proof of negligence is required. No prior bite history is needed. California rejected the one-bite rule. This is one of the strongest dog bite liability standards in the country.
What if the owner says I provoked the dog?+
Provocation is a defense in California, but the standard is high. Normal actions, including walking past a dog, approaching it, or making eye contact, do not constitute provocation. The defense requires intentional, deliberate abuse or teasing of the animal. We challenge provocation defenses when the evidence does not support them, including gathering witness statements about exactly what occurred.
