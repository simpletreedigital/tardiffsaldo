Estate Planning for Morro Bay Residents

Coastal property values in Morro Bay and the surrounding area have appreciated significantly. Homes with bay views or ocean access regularly exceed $900,000, and properties in Cayucos and on the Baywood Park waterfront frequently sell above $1 million. For any coastal homeowner, a living trust is the most important legal document they can have. California probate on an estate of that value is public, takes over a year, and costs tens of thousands of dollars. Our flat-fee trust packages are available to Morro Bay clients with free consultations by phone or video.

Most everyone needs a trust. We have litigated poorly written estate plans in court, and that experience shapes every document we create. See our trusts and estate planning page and our living trusts and wills page for the complete picture. Do Morro Bay property owners need a living trust?+
In most cases, yes. Any California resident who owns real estate should seriously consider a living trust to avoid probate. California probate applies to estates above $184,500 in individual-name assets, and the statutory fees are calculated on gross asset value, not equity. A $900,000 home with a $400,000 mortgage is still a $900,000 probate asset. Given current Morro Bay and the SLO Coastal Area property values, most homeowners are well above that threshold. We offer flat-fee trust packages and free consultations.
