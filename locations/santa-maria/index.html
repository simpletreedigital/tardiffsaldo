Common Questions

FAQs for Santa Maria Clients

Does Tardiff and Saldo serve Santa Maria clients?+
Yes. We serve Santa Maria and the surrounding North Santa Barbara County communities from our San Luis Obispo office. The city is approximately 25 miles south of SLO via Highway 101. Most consultations are conducted by phone or video, and in-person appointments for document signing are available at our SLO office. We also handle probate matters filed in Santa Barbara County Superior Court for local estates.
Which court handles probate for Santa Maria residents?+
Santa Maria is in Santa Barbara County, so estate matters are filed in Santa Barbara County Superior Court at 312 East Cook Street in Santa Maria. This branch is the most convenient for North County residents. We handle Santa Barbara County probate proceedings and are familiar with the local filing requirements and procedural differences from SLO County.
Does Tardiff and Saldo handle cross-county estates?+
Yes, and this is a common situation for families with property on both sides of the SLO and Santa Barbara County line. Agricultural land near Nipomo and the Nipomo Mesa frequently crosses the county boundary. A single properly structured living trust eliminates the need for two separate probate proceedings in each county, which can otherwise become a costly and time-consuming process.
Can I complete an estate plan without driving to San Luis Obispo?+
Your consultation and document review can be completed entirely by phone or video. The only step that typically requires an in-person appointment is the document signing, which takes place at our SLO office.
