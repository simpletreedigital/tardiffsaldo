Estate Planning for San Luis Obispo Residents

San Luis Obispo property values have appreciated dramatically over the past decade. For any SLO homeowner, that valuation creates significant probate exposure if no trust is in place. California statutory probate fees on a $1 million estate exceed $46,000 in combined attorney and executor fees alone, before court costs, appraisals, and publication. Do San Luis Obispo property owners need a living trust?+
In most cases, yes. Any California resident who owns real estate should seriously consider a living trust to avoid probate. California probate applies to estates above $184,500 in individual-name assets, and the statutory fees are calculated on gross asset value, not equity. A $900,000 home with a $400,000 mortgage is still a $900,000 probate asset. Given current San Luis Obispo and SLO County property values, most homeowners are well above that threshold. We offer flat-fee trust packages and free consultations.
